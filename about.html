Mega Star



Mammootty, born Muhammad Kutty Panaparambil Ismail on September 7, 1951, is a renowned Indian actor and film producer, predominantly working in the Malayalam film industry. Widely recognized as one of the greatest actors in Indian cinema, he has a prolific career spanning five decades, appearing in over 400 films, primarily in lead roles. Mammootty was raised in a middle-class Muslim family. His father, Ismail, ran a garment and rice business and also farmed rice, while his mother, Fatima, was a homemaker. He is the eldest of six siblings. He earned a Bachelor's degree from Maharajah's College, Ernakulam, and later a Law degree (LL.B.) from Government Law College in Ernakulam. He practiced law for two years in Manjeri before pursuing his passion for acting.
Mammootty has received numerous awards, including 3 National Film Awards, 11 Kerala State Film Awards, 11 Kerala Film Critics Awards, and 15 Filmfare Awards South. He was awarded the Padma Shri in 1998 and received honorary doctorates from the University of Kerala and the University of Calicut in 2010. CNN recognized him as one of the "men who changed the face of the Indian Cinema". Mammootty is involved in philanthropy through the Care & Share International Foundation, which supports healthcare and education. He is also a patron of the Pain and Palliative Care Society and advocates for social justice.
Mammootty, an iconic figure in Indian cinema, particularly in the Malayalam film industry, boasts a career spanning over five decades and encompassing more than 400 films. His enduring appeal extends beyond his impressive filmography, stemming from his dedication to his craft, his willingness to experiment with diverse roles, and his significant contributions both on and off screen.
